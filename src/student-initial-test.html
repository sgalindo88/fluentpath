20 marks  ·  3 tasks

💬 Record your spoken answers using the microphone button below each question. You can also use the notes field to plan before speaking. Your teacher will be listening on the video call.
Part A  ·  Introduction
Question 21
Tell me a little about yourself — where you're from, what you do, and what you enjoy doing in your free time.
Speak for 1–2 minutes. Try to give extended answers, not just one-word replies.
Part A  ·  Everyday Topics
Question 22
Describe a person in your life who has had an important influence on you. How have they influenced you?
Use descriptive language and a mix of past and present tenses.
Part B  ·  Extended Speaking (2 minutes)
Question 23  ·  1 minute to prepare
Talk about a place you have visited or would like to visit. Describe what it is like, explain why it is (or would be) meaningful to you, and say whether you would recommend it to others.
Organise your ideas: describe → why it matters → recommendation.
Part C  ·  Discussion & Opinion
Question 24
Some people think that university education should be free for everyone. Others believe students should pay for it themselves. What do you think, and why?
Give your opinion clearly, support it with reasons, and be ready for follow-up questions from the examiner.
